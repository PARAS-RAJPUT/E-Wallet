The Privacy Problem We're Solving

Today's Reality

  • Repeating sizes at every store visit
  • Forgetting dietary restrictions at restaurants
  • Sharing too much personal data unnecessarily
  • No control over who sees your information
  • Data scattered across platforms

Hushh's Approach

  • Single source of truth in your wallet
  • Granular consent controls per interaction
  • Minimal disclosure by default
  • Full audit trail of data access
  • Standards-based verifiable credentials

The Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 mandates purpose-limited processing, explicit consent, and data subject rights. Hushh bakes these principles into the architecture from day one, ensuring compliance isn't an afterthought but a foundational feature that builds user trust.

Core Architecture: How It Works

  1. 01 Create Your Cards

    Users build preference cards through intuitive forms, importing data from past purchases or verified sources like DigiLocker for identity verification.

  2. 02 Store in Wallet

    Cards become Apple .pkpass or Google Wallet passes with QR codes containing short-lived tokens, not raw personal data.

  3. 03 Scan & Consent

    When scanned, users see exactly what's requested and choose to share Basic, Standard, or Detailed levelsâ€”or deny entirely.

  4. 04 Selective Disclosure

    Only requested claims are revealed using W3C Verifiable Credentials and SD-JWT, keeping everything else private.

  5. 05 Audit & Revoke

    Full transparency log shows who saw what and when, with one-tap revocation that instantly disables access.
